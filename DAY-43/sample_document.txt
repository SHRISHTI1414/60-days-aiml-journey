Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a technique that combines retrieval systems with Large Language Models.

RAG systems retrieve relevant context from external knowledge sources before generating responses.

Embeddings are numerical vector representations of text that capture semantic meaning.

Vector databases like FAISS are used for efficient similarity search across embeddings.

Chunking helps divide large documents into smaller contextual segments for retrieval.

Retrieval quality directly impacts the accuracy and grounding of generated responses.
